Trust relocates as proof gets costly

proofprocess
publisherssocietiesjournals universitieslibrariesed-techintergov

Judge a checkable artifact — a paper, a citation — and proof is cheap. Trust rides on the work.
Judge a diffuse outcome — access, equity, governance — and there's nothing to inspect. Trust rides on who's accountable.
AI widens the gap. Once a machine does the work, the reasoning is obscured inside the model — so trust slides from proof to process.

When proof becomes impossible, trust doesn't vanish — it becomes governance.
We trust machines exactly as far as we can check them — past that, it's faith.
Humans get trust on credit — extended ahead, repaid later. Machines pay cash — proof up front.
“Humans trust ahead.
Machines trust in reverse.”
